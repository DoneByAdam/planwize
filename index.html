About & security

What Planwize is, who's behind it, and exactly how your data is handled.

What makes Planwize different

  • Paycheck-level precision. Most calculators give you a yearly number. Planwize models every single paycheck — weekly, biweekly, semimonthly, or monthly — so it catches things others miss, like exactly which pay period hits your IRS limit and how much employer match that costs you.
  • Federal + state, not just federal. Approximate state income tax for all 50 states + DC, including quirks like Pennsylvania taxing pre-tax 401(k) contributions.
  • Named, dated plan history. Run what-ifs, model a second job, or keep last year's numbers as a permanent record — not a single form that overwrites itself.
  • Explains itself. An 18-term glossary and a coaching corner turn "the tool told me a number" into "I understand why."
  • Zero-knowledge option. Add a passphrase and even Planwize's own database can't read your numbers. Most free tools don't offer this.
  • No ads, no data resale, ever. If a paid tier exists, that's the entire business model — not your data.

Security architecture

🔒
In transit

Every connection uses TLS/HTTPS — from your browser to Planwize's servers, always encrypted.

🗄️
At rest

Saved plans live in a database encrypted at rest (AES-256), access-controlled so a plan can only ever be read or written by the account that owns it — enforced by the database itself, not just application code.

🔑
Optional zero-knowledge encryption

Set a passphrase and your plan is encrypted in your browser (AES-256-GCM, PBKDF2, 210,000 iterations) before it ever leaves your device. The server stores only ciphertext it cannot decrypt. Trade-off: lose the passphrase and the data is unrecoverable — by design, nobody holds a spare key.

🧪
Try before you trust

The entire tool works with no account at all — your numbers stay on your device until you decide otherwise.

🚫
No ad trackers

No ad networks, no third-party trackers riding along with your financial data.
